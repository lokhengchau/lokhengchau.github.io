About Me

I am currently a second year Master's student in Measurement, Evaluation, and Research Methedology (MERM) at the University of British Columbia (UBC) in BC, Canada. Before this, I worked as an public elementary school teacher in Hong Kong. Though I enjoyed my years of teaching tremendously, I grew curious in test development and educational measurement and statistics, and hence my decision to move to Vancouver. My interests also include quantitative research methods, data visualization, and education.

MERM Research Tools

The MERM Research Tools project started in the Fall of 2017. The site contains many Shiny R web applications my colleagues and I developed with the goal to provide easy access and a point-and-click interface for quick and basic results from item analysis under classical test theory and exploratory factor analysis, etc. This site also contains many interactive learning tools and visualizations for statistical concepts. Feel free to check it out and leave us comments for improvement or future projects you would like to see. The code to some of the Shiny applications are also available on my github repo.
